Our Ethos

Provincial Research Data Services exists because Alberta's health data belongs, ultimately, to Albertans. Every record we steward represents a person's encounter with the health system, entrusted to us with the expectation that it will be protected and used only in ways that serve the public good. That trust is the foundation of everything we do.

We draw a deliberate distinction between data access and data accessibility. Access is permission — the gate through which approved researchers pass. Accessibility is capability — the proactive design of our data, documentation, and services so that information can be found, understood, and meaningfully used by researchers regardless of their technical, statistical, or analytical skill set. Granting access without building accessibility leaves data technically available but practically out of reach. Our work is to close that gap: we remove expertise-based barriers so that a clinician with a question, a graduate student with an idea, and a seasoned epidemiologist with a program of research can each turn Alberta's data holdings into rigorous, trustworthy insight.

We serve many interests, and when they pull in different directions, we know how to decide. The people of Alberta come first — it is their data, and their interests are the measure against which every choice is tested. We are obligated next to the health service and its people, who transform discovery to change; then to the researchers who turn data into discovery; then to the institutions whose partnership gives research its rigour and continuity; and to our funders, whose support we value and are accountable for, but which follows from doing valuable work rather than defining it. This is not a statement of who matters — all of these partners matter — it is a statement of how we act when we cannot satisfy everyone at once.

Our purpose is simple to state and demanding to deliver: we leverage routinely collected health information to improve care, and we improve data accessibility to drive innovation. Health data has value only when it can be responsibly used — data locked away is safe but sterile; data carelessly released is useful but corrosive to public trust. We hold both obligations at once, ensuring that privacy, security, and the principles of the health information stewardship are never the price of progress.

We are also impatient. It is said to take seventeen years for a discovery to become standard of care, and no single team can close that gap alone — but we refuse to treat it as fixed. We do our part with each project, to shorten the distance between a research question and a trustworthy answer that changes the system and improves care. We do our part structurally: by being close to the healthcare delivery system rather than standing outside it, we shorten the path from evidence to adoption, because findings generated inside the system are findings the system is already positioned to act on. Every year taken off that timeline is a year sooner that evidence reaches the bedside.

We hold ourselves to the standards of both science and stewardship. Our analyses are reproducible, our methods transparent, our advice honest — including when the honest answer is that the data cannot support the question being asked. The data we manage is not ours; the discoveries it enables are not ours to claim. Our role is to make others' work possible, and we consider that role essential, not subordinate.

Why do we do all of this? To make things better. Better care for patients, better tools for the health system, better answers for researchers — and a health system that learns from every person it serves.
